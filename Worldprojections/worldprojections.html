In this project I learned how to display images in different projections

Describe in your own words how you displayed the map in different projections using QGIS

I opened a country basemap vector file in QGIS. I then installed the plugin Indicatrix Mapper, which plots cirlces on a map that display the distortion of the projection by varying in shape and size. I changed projections by selecting the ESRI tab in the bottom right corner and entering the ESPG code corresponding to the desired projection. After generating the map, I moved to map composer and formatted the map, then exporting it as a png.

WGS84 Projection

Overall country shapes preserved. Poles are heavily distorted, Antarctica and Greenland are larger than in reality, while countries in the center of the map are smaller.

Aitoff Projection

Variation in size is lesser than in the WGS84. However, latitude lines curve outward rather than in, which is counterintuitive for a sphere or ellipsoid.

Pseudo Mercator Projection

Easily displayable and readable on a rectangular plane. However, north and south are much larger than center countries. Greenland is the size of Africa.

Sphere Winkel I Projection

Size is comparable for most of the middle of the Earth, only becoming larger at the outermost latitudes. The curvature of the projection is more digestable than Aitoff.

Sinusoidal Projection

Africa and Europe appear relatively undistorted. However, Asia, North America, South America, and Australia appear curved. Sizes are comparable between low and high latitudes.

Equidistant Conic Projection

For most of the habitable world, distortion is comparable. Only Antarctica is severely distorted. The top-down perspective is disorienting.

Cassini Projection

Other than Afirca, nearly incomprehensible. The very center of the map displays circles with very similar sizes while drastically changing size as the edges of the map are approached.

Cylindrical Equidistant Projection

Rectangular and easy to read plane, relatively equal sphere sizes, except poles seem squished.

North Pole Azimuthal Projection

Antarcica wraps around the Earth. Other than Antarctica, most countries have accurate comparative sizes. However, they're all too small to distinguish well.
